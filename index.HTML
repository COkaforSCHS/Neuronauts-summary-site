the Camp

NeuroNauts is a 3-week hybrid summer enrichment camp designed for gifted learners in grades 7–11. The camp blends creative problem-solving, interdisciplinary inquiry, and socio-emotional learning to cultivate minds that think deeper, stretch farther, and dream wilder.

Cognitive Development

Deepen creative and critical thinking across disciplines.

Affective Development

Provide a supportive, inclusive community where gifted learners can connect and collaborate.

Identity and Leadership

Nurture identity development, self-efficacy, and leadership among students with advanced cognitive abilities.

Itinerary

Week 1: Ignite

Workshops: Creative Cognition, Emotional Intelligence, Design Thinking

Team Challenge: Design a device to solve a local problem

SEL Focus: Identity & Empathy

Week 2: Expand

Tracks: Literary Worlds & Future Mythmaking, Planet Hackers: Global Sustainability Game, Persona & Performance

Guest Speakers: GHP alums, Duke instructors, and a Davidson Fellow

SEL Focus: Mindfulness & Stress Mastery

Week 3: Launch

Capstone: TED-style talk or prototype pitch at “NeuroNauts Summit”

Community Expo: Parents, local educators, and industry mentors attend

SEL Focus: Resilience & Goal Setting

Cost and Scholarships

In-Person: $1,750 (includes lodging, meals, materials, & excursions)

Virtual Cohort: $400 (includes mailed project kit + access to daily workshops)

Scholarships: Available via Davidson Institute and GAGC partnerships.

Application Process

Eligibility: Open to students in grades 7–11

Requirements: Must meet one or more of the following:

  • Identified as gifted by district standards
  • PSAT ≥ 90th percentile or Duke TIP eligibility
  • Nomination by educator or counselor + creative problem-solving portfolio

Application Deadline: May 15

Notification: Rolling Admissions (Final notifications by June 1)
